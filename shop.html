Case Study E-Commerce Shop iOS App Web 2023 2 min

Situation

  • The client's e-commerce store had a lot of visitors and clicks, but the conversion rate has been dropping for months. The following tasks were set to define and resolve the problems:
  • Review the current UX (user experience) design and UI (user interface) usability;
  • Conduct user research and analyze customer behavior data to evaluate the effectiveness of marketing campaigns;
  • Design new UI and improve UX usability.

User research, UX research and usability testing

  • 28% of customers left the store after adding items to the cart;
  • 11% of unique visitors left the shop due to long page loading;
  • The product's UI was overloaded, which led to a broken UX;
  • Non-intuitive interactions forced users to take actions by trial and error, trying to complete their tasks;

Data analysis

  • Confusing first impressions, outdated UX & UI performance with slow loading - caused frustration and customer outflow. Previous UI is under NDA, but data is open:
  • Conversions dropped for over 9 months despite accurate pricing for the target audience;
  • Unoptimized UI elements with heavy scripts reduced performance and made navigation difficult;
  • Annoying pop-ups and notifications drove away 22% of customers.

Prototyping and Final Design

  • The solution emerges from data research: to develop a clean, functional, and lightweight UI/UX design for an enjoyable marketplace experience.
  • After feedback on Figma prototypes, I created the final design of the lightest version. Below, I will showcase some pages with new simple UI.

Products

  • Main Page with infinite scroll. Nothing more, the products occupy the whole screen. Nice!
  • Search hides on scroll down and reappears on scroll up;
  • Logic-based navigation through gestures and browser controls - no need for an additional UI;
  • Ads can be placed as native items - so users won't be annoyed by pop-ups;
  • Zero distraction, only shopping.

Product Page

  • Choose product options, read reviews, and view special offers.
  • Legit Reviews: customers invited to review purchased items after one week;
  • AI-optimized product photos provide high quality presentation in under 90KB per image;
  • Lightning-fast loading - with an input delay of under 100ms, the page content loads in 2.5sec;
  • I greatly optimized the code to score 92 points on Google PageSpeed and get to the green zone in Google's Core Web Vitals rating system.

Cart

  • Users can easily search, evaluate and manage items:
  • More than 70% of shoppers searched for items from the cart page directly;
  • Very important page. No crazy menu, lists and buttons. Cart must be intuitively simple, or Your customers will get mad;
  • The related products list also works well here - about 5% of customers added suggested items to the cart at checkout;
  • Search from the cart is very effective user-oriented functional element that boosted conversion by 18%.

Summary

  • The customers have limited battery and limited shopping time. So the store probably, does not need that heavy effects, distracting banners and nasty animations.
  • Instant loading, clean and user-centric UX/UI design have profound impact on the success of a digital product. Make the shopping experience enjoyable and don't disturb customers from buying.
